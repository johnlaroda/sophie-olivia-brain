## Sizing guidance
**Known issue — our garments run about 2 sizes SMALL for our target market.**
Our supplier (DayOne) is China-based and manufactures to Asian sizing, which is
roughly two sizes smaller than the Western/EU/US sizing our customers expect.
This is our single biggest source of fit complaints and returns.

Practical guidance for support replies:
- When a customer says an item is too small / too tight, this is expected
  behaviour, not a defect. Validate it kindly and steer toward the right size.
- Recommend customers size UP (typically by 2) versus their usual size.
- Until the size chart is corrected, set expectations proactively where possible.

**Size charts:** Most products have a size chart on their individual Shopify
product page — check the specific product page for the customer's item first.
Some products don't yet have a chart: those are still being fixed while we wait
for correct measurements from the supplier (DayOne). If a customer asks about
sizing for a product with no chart, give the general size-up guidance above and,
if needed, flag for John rather than quoting measurements we don't have.

## Common fit complaints & standard answers
These are draft standard answers built around the "runs ~2 sizes small" issue.
Always adapt to the specific ticket and stay within policy (see context/policies.md).

1. "It's too small / too tight / doesn't fit."
   → Acknowledge warmly. Explain our pieces tend to run smaller than typical
     EU/US sizing, so we usually recommend going up ~2 sizes. Offer the exchange
     path (store credit within 14 days of delivery — see policies). Help them
     pick the right size.

2. "The band is too tight."
   → Same root cause (Asian sizing). Recommend sizing up; offer exchange via
     store credit. If they report the band quality is faulty (not just fit),
     treat as a possible quality issue and flag for John / document for DayOne.

3. "The cup is too small / gaping."
   → If too small: size-up guidance + exchange. If gaping (too big in cup),
     that's a genuine fit mismatch — guide to the correct size and offer exchange.

4. "I ordered my usual size and it doesn't fit."
   → Reassure this is a known sizing difference, not their error. Apologise for
     the inconvenience, explain the size-up guidance, and make the exchange easy.

5. "Is this a defect or just the size?"
   → If purely fit/size → handle as sizing (above). If material/stitching/
     construction fault → quality issue: flag for John and document for DayOne
     in Slack #dayone-quality (drafted; John posts).

> Note: this is a starting framework — refine with John's preferred wording over time.
